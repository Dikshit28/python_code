def degree_polynomial(degree):
    poly_reg = PolynomialFeatures(degree = degree)
    x_poly = poly_reg.fit_transform(x)
    poly_reg.fit(x_poly, y)
    lin_reg_2 = LinearRegression()
    lin_reg_2.fit(x_poly, y)
    print(f"Poly reg with degree {degree} prediction",lin_reg_2.predict(poly_reg.fit_transform([[6.5]])))

    plt.scatter(x, y, color = 'red')
    plt.plot(x, lin_reg_2.predict(poly_reg.fit_transform(x)), color = 'blue')
    plt.title('Truth or Bluff (Polynomial Regression)')
    plt.xlabel('Position level')
    plt.ylabel('Salary')
    plt.show()

Conclusion¶

  • Simple Linear Regression is just polyomial regression with degree 1
  • Polynomial regression is a generalization of linear regression
  • For degree 4 and above the model may not be very accurate for test data (due to overfitting)
  • Keeping the degree of polynomial regression low will help in model accuracy
